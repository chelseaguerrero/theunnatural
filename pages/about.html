About

The Story – The Unnatural by David Eagleman, found in the book SUM: Forty Tales of the Afterlife, is a short story about you, the reader, that narrates your choice when given the opportunity to change anything and live life over again. Your character chooses immortality, but then finds out that this was not what you bargained for. Instead of happy immortal human beings, people ended up miserable and planned their own suicides. The story then ends similarly the way it began.
Design – Compared to the last project, I wanted to do something different, less minimal. So right away, I opted for cursive fonts for the logo/header and a few subheadings. The cursive font represented life and how we have our ups and our downs. With the background image, I honestly could not think of one, but then I started looking through some of my pictures I took last quarter and I came across the current background. I liked it because it went with the color scheme, which was pretty subtle already and included other cool hues. The image I chose represents life and the afterlife. The city with the lights symbolizes life, while up in the sky represents the almost reaching the afterlife, but not quite. This is because the character, for the majority of the story has not necessarily entered the afterlife as once he/she died they were returned to life on Earth after making their decision.
Layout – The interactive aspect of the website is from rereading the short story. It was the part where there is a, “pamphlet [that] suggests that you might choose to make yourself two inches taller, or give everyone on the Earth a better sense of humor. Or make birds talk.” (Eagleman 52). After reading this, I wanted to further emphasize on these options the pamphlet suggested, since in the story the character goes for immortality. Also, the approach I used to make this website was for it to be humorous, especially the headers of these pages (tall, humor, and birds).
